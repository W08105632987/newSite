Our Journey

The MonieKing Story

2017

The Beginning

MonieKing was founded in Bwari, Abuja, by Mr. Kenneth (Director of Wizzellard Concept) and Mr. Joshua (Director of Joshline Fund Management). Two entrepreneurs from different backgrounds united by a common vision: bringing practical banking and savings solutions closer to local communities.

At a time when many Nigerians faced major challenges accessing reliable cash services, fair savings options, and affordable credit, we set out to answer three critical questions:

  • How do we bring banking services closer to everyday people?
  • How do we offer reliable, quality cash services without stress?
  • How do we help people save and access loans conveniently, without excessive charges?
2020

Building Trust from the Ground Up

We started as a POS-driven cash services business, strategically placing POS points within communities to ensure customers could withdraw cash anytime. Beyond cash dispensing, we supported customers with structured savings, including community-focused food contribution packs.

The early years weren't easy. Fake contribution schemes were widespread, and many people had suffered financial losses. Trust was fragile. What sustained us was consistency ‚Äî showing up every day, physically present at business locations, identifiable through customized branded shirts, and always available to customers.

Savings were paid out reliably, including agreed returns, and commitments were honored without excuses. Over time, people began to notice the difference. Trust grew ‚Äî not through marketing promises, but through visible presence, reliability, and consistency.

2023

Growth & Strategic Partnerships

As trust deepened, we expanded our services to meet growing community needs. We secured our first major aggregator partnership with OPay, followed by Paga, significantly strengthening our infrastructure and credibility.

Our services expanded to include:

  • NIN enrollment and resolution of NIN-related issues
  • BVN enrollment services
  • POS aggregation and device sales
2024

Digital Transformation

We explored digital expansion through an early app development initiative, gaining valuable insights that shaped our future strategy. Despite industry-wide disruptions ‚Äî including Nigeria's cashless policy era ‚Äî MonieKing remained resilient, adapted quickly, and continued to serve customers without interruption.

2025

The Cooperative Future

We launched our most ambitious project yet: a cooperative savings fintech application (CoopHub), developed in collaboration with Wema Bank. This marked our strategic shift toward full digital transformation and formal cooperative operations.

We officially adopted the cooperative model, recognizing it as the structure that best aligns with our core values:

  • Members are stakeholders, not just users
  • Savings structured for long-term financial discipline
  • Profits shared transparently through annual dividends
  • Fair access to loans at minimal interest (5%)
  • Collective strength replaces individual vulnerability

Today, we serve over 2,000 active customers.

2027+

Our Future

By 2027, MonieKing aims to operate as a fully digital cooperative platform, while maintaining the community trust that built our foundation. We're committed to helping Nigerians ‚Äî students, business owners, workers, and families ‚Äî secure their money, plan ahead, and achieve meaningful financial goals, together.
